From Philadelphia to
Puerto Rico

After Hurricane Maria, Chef José Esteban Rivero and his wife Sarah Santos returned to Puerto Rico with a mission: to support local agriculture and create a gathering place that celebrates the island's incredible bounty.

Founded in April 2019, PROLE Cocina & Barra is more than a restaurant. It's a commitment to the "De la Finca a la Mesa" (Farm-to-Table) movement, a celebration of resilience, and a testament to the power of community.

With 11 years of training in Philadelphia's finest kitchens, Chef José brings international techniques to showcase Puerto Rico's seasonal ingredients, creating a menu that evolves every few days based on what's freshest from our local farmers.

Meet Our Farmers

Fresh. Seasonal. Local.

Our menu changes every two to three days, ensuring that every ingredient is at its peak. We work directly with Puerto Rican farmers and producers, building relationships that strengthen our community and guarantee the highest quality on your plate.

This isn't just a philosophy—it's our promise to you and to the island we call home.

Supporting Our Community

Every dish at PROLE tells a story that begins in the fields and farms of Puerto Rico. We work directly with local producers, creating relationships that go beyond transactions.

This commitment means our menu is a true reflection of what's growing right now, supporting the agricultural economy of our island while ensuring unmatched freshness and flavor.

When you dine at PROLE, you're not just enjoying a meal—you're participating in a movement to strengthen Puerto Rico's food system and celebrate its agricultural heritage.
